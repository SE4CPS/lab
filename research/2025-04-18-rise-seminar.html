RQ1: Reddit-Enriched Release Notes

Enriched notes per component:

  • Android: 1000
  • Linux: 200
  • iOS: 149
  • Kotlin: 51
  • Windows: 22
  • ReactJs: 16
  • Swift: 8
  • Java: 8
  • NodeJs: 3

Most posts lacked version info; Android & Java were exceptions. OS posts (Jun–Jul 2024) were active;

RQ2: Categorization & Breaking Updates

Broader Categorization

  • Android: 6 → 12
  • iOS: 5 → 6
  • Linux: 1 → 12

Breaking Updates

  • "Freezing on iOS 17.5.1"
  • "Bluetooth not working"
  • "Phone won't turn on"
RQ3: OS Impact & Versioning Insights

Feedback Variation

  • iOS: 13/105 posts were breaking (avg = 5)
  • Mobile updates = high (clinical) risk

Versioning Matches

iOS posts had the most version info.

RQ4: Update Type & Reddit Feedback

Versioning Visibility

  • Android: 50%+ posts include major versions
  • Java & iOS users share detailed patches

Tech Component Gaps

  • Kotlin & React: fewer version details
  • Likely moved to GitHub platform
RQ5: Common User Concerns on Reddit

Frequent Issues

  • WiFi, Bluetooth, config problems
  • Phone-wide bugs, not app-specific
  • Often missing in app store reviews

Concern Types

  • Usability, performance, compatibility
  • Security—only if user is affected
RQ6: Reddit Data Impact on Critical Issue Detection

Increased Detection

  • 58 breaking issues identified
  • Highlights future legal impacts

Impact on Clinical Applications

  • Early identification improves mitigation
  • Absent from release notes/CVEs
  • Potential for better user experience
